SchoolSaaS — User Tutorial

Step-by-step visual guide for school staff. Each section shows a snapshot of the screen followed by instructions on how to use it.

1. Logging In

Every school user logs in from the same school portal. You will need your school code, email address, and password — provided by your school owner or headmaster.

http://localhost:5173/login

SchoolSaaS

  1. 1
    Open your browser and go to the school portal URL (e.g. http://localhost:5173/login or the live domain your school uses).
  2. 2
    Enter your School Code exactly as given by your administrator (e.g. BFA001). This is case-sensitive.
  3. 3
    Enter your Email and Password.
  4. 4
    Click Sign In. On success you are taken to your dashboard.
If login fails, double-check your school code first — it is the most common mistake. Contact your school owner if the account is locked.
Teachers and finance staff use the same login page. Do NOT use the /admin/login URL — that is for platform-level administrators only.

7. Taking Attendance

Attendance is recorded class-by-class. Select a class and date, then mark each student as Present, Absent, Late, or Excused. You can also bulk-mark the whole class at once.

    Select the Class from the dropdown. The student list loads automatically.
  2. 2
    The Date defaults to today. Change it to record attendance for a past or future date.
  3. 3
    For each student, click the appropriate status button: present, absent, late, or excused. The selected button is highlighted.

Roles that can record attendance: Owner, Teacher, Class Teacher, Headmaster (Academics), Headmaster (Admin). Other roles can view but not edit.

8. Entering Grades

The Grades page lets teachers enter scores for a class-subject-term combination. There are three score columns per student: Class Assignment (/30), Test Score (/30), and Exam Score (/40), totalling 100.

12. Posting & Reading Announcements

Announcements are broadcast notices posted to specific audiences: all staff, teachers only, heads and leaders, or for students. Anyone in the audience can read them; only authorised roles can post.

  1. 1
    Click + Post Announcement to open the compose form.
  2. 2
    Enter a Title and optional body text.
  3. 3
    Choose the Audience: All Staff, Staff Only, Teachers, Heads / Leaders, or For Students. Your role limits which audiences you can post to.
  4. 4
    Optionally set an Expiry Date — the announcement dims and shows "Expired" after that date.
  5. 5
    Click Post. The announcement appears at the top of the list for all users in the audience.
  6. 6
    Owners can also Archive or Delete any announcement using the buttons on its card.
Roles that can post: Owner, Headmaster (Academics & Admin), Department Head (to teachers only), Teacher and Class Teacher (to students only).
